Powder Metallurgy Applications for Precision Mechanical Parts

Powder metallurgy is widely used to manufacture precision metal components for automotive, motorcycle, industrial machinery, power tools, EV systems, and automation equipment. Yeh Sheng produces custom Sintered Products including shock absorber components, oil pump rotors, gears, bushings, sprockets, and structural parts for OEM applications. We leverage our advanced Manufacturing Capabilities and streamlined Service Workflow to deliver high-quality, cost-effective solutions.

Automotive & Motorcycle

Powder metallurgy is widely used in automotive and motorcycle systems where dimensional consistency, wear resistance, and cost-effective production are required. Yeh Sheng manufactures sintered components for suspension, transmission, oil pump, and engine-related applications.

Key Applications

Shock absorber components, oil pump rotors, timing sprockets, transmission parts, ABS sensor rings, and structural PM components.

Advantages

High material utilization, stable dimensional control, good wear resistance, and suitability for medium to high-volume production.

Related PM Parts

Sintered shock absorber parts, oil pump gears and rotors, sprockets, bushings, and custom automotive PM parts.

Industrial Machinery & Mechanical Systems

Industrial machinery requires durable metal components that can withstand continuous operation, friction, and mechanical load. Powder metallurgy provides a reliable solution for gears, bushings, cams, and structural parts used in automation and production equipment.

Key Applications

Conveyor system gears, hydraulic pump parts, textile machinery components, bushings, couplings, and mechanical drive parts.

Advantages

Good wear resistance, repeatable production quality, reduced machining waste, and stable performance in industrial environments.

Related PM Parts

Sintered gears, bushings, cams, flanges, couplings, and custom machinery components.

Robotics & Automation

Robotics and automation systems require compact, precise, and reliable mechanical parts. Powder metallurgy is suitable for producing small gears, structural components, and motion-related parts used in robotic arms, actuators, and automated equipment.

Key Applications

Robot arm components, actuator parts, small precision gears, motion control components, and automation system parts.

Advantages

Stable dimensions, compact part design, material efficiency, and suitability for customized mechanical components.

Related PM Parts

PM gears, structural parts, bushings, spacers, sleeves, and custom sintered components for automation equipment.

Power Tools

Power tools require compact, high-strength components that can handle torque, impact, and repeated use. Powder metallurgy is commonly used for internal drive parts and wear-resistant mechanical components in handheld tools.

Key Applications

Planetary gear systems, drill chuck components, impact blocks, gearbox parts, and tool transmission components.

Advantages

High strength, good wear resistance, efficient mass production, and the ability to form complex geometries with reduced machining.

Related PM Parts

Sintered gears, tool drive components, impact parts, bushings, and custom PM power tool parts.

EV & Electrification

Electric vehicles and green energy systems require efficient, durable, and lightweight metal components. Powder metallurgy can support motor-related parts, magnetic components, and mechanical parts used in EV and renewable energy applications.

Key Applications

Motor components, inner & outer rotors, soft magnetic components, precision drive components, and electrification systems.

Advantages

Material efficiency, design flexibility, stable production quality, and suitability for precision components in energy-related systems.

Related PM Parts

Hardware & Precision Components

Powder metallurgy is suitable for producing small, complex, and repeatable metal components used in hardware, mechanical assemblies, and precision systems. It is especially useful when parts require consistent dimensions and reduced secondary machining.

Key Applications

Lock components, hinges, small mechanical parts, sleeves, spacers, connectors, and precision structural components.

Advantages

Net-shape manufacturing, consistent quality, reduced material waste, and cost-effective production for repeated part designs.

Related PM Parts

Sleeves, spacers, small gears, bushings, structural parts, and custom sintered metal components.
